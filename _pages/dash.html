Daily regimen

This is ideal, but feel free to pull audibles and work on whatever you think it is best to work on, but if you can't think of anything or you are lost, do this.

Week day (MON-THU)

  • Wake up and study RL or DL (reading book or implementing) for 2 hours (8-10am)
  • Work on schoolwork from 10am-7pm.
  • Other projects that are important (ad hoc)
  • Get to the gym to climb, and maybe lift and run, every Tuesday at approx 8 or 9 pm.

Weekend (FRI-SUN)

  • Never work on any school work if you can manage (starting to maybe rethink this a bit)
  • Work on most important project and try to save large chunks of time for working
  • Saturday: research
  • Sunday: robotics team

Short-term concrete projects

  1. Working on research project in Hermans lab. Implementing GPS and getting it to work on the real robot
  2. Tuning rock detector project and getting it to really work. Until you run out of things to improve.
  3. Implement RL algos (basics to PPO, ES) from scratch (in np and tf) to learn them well
  4. Read through the Goodfellow and Sutton books
  5. Working on software for robotics club (simulation, high-level autonomy, sensors)
  6. Produce a simple document to give lay of the land on how to get started with DL or RL, gym
  7. Contributing to hypothes.is to fix that one bug and add notes to mine or the whole thing
  8. Bullet modder (at least for textures)?

Anti-priorities

These things should specifically not be done. They have been shown to waste a lot of time and a net negative effect on progress. Unless actions are pertinent to one of the priorities, they should not be done. It is good to explore a bit, but not much. Doing the direct thing that is a priority is the most important. You don't learn to write code by reading about it. You learn by actually writing code. You have to dedicate specific time to it. You should be able to learn to do these things without having to "expand your horizons". For example, in talking to people, you don't need to know everything going on at this point; you just need to be interested in them. That is all you need to do.
